About us

Art, learning, AI. Minus the hype.

We make things that help people get on with AI — shows, classes, the odd workshop. The goal is for it to feel useful and a bit beautiful, and not like a sales pitch.

What we actually do sits between AI-inspired art, education, and public-facing events — meant for communities, schools, organisations, and anyone who's curious but a little tired of the noise.

We're a handful of Singapore-based finance and tech people — "young seniors", if you like — who've put money into local startups and gotten some out. We started Sagelion partly so we'd stay close to where culture, technology, and markets are actually going, and partly because we want other Singaporeans (especially the ones feeling squeezed by AI) to navigate this stretch of the digital economy with taste, rigour, and a bit of heart.

Community work. Slow, useful, hopefully built to last.

The gap.

Everyone's heard about AI. Hardly anyone's using it properly. The setup's a mess, the workflows break halfway through, and there's just too much friction in the way.

What you'll walk out with.

  • Foundation models (GPT, Gemini, Kimi) → agent frameworks (OpenClaw, LangChain, n8n) → actual business uses.
  • The difference between generative AI (which makes stuff) and agentic AI (which goes off and does stuff).
  • A full stack — from free public tools to no-code automation you can run in production.

Pain we actually help with.

  • PMETs drowning in reports.
  • Teachers buried in admin.
  • Retirees who feel a bit left behind.

Things we've actually shipped.

  • Nejilock Property AI — proof of concept.
  • FlowDaptor Operating Backbone.
  • Sagelion Grant Engine.

Security, not an afterthought.

Human in the loop, less data than you think, proper audit trails, PDPA-friendly. We're not interested in cowboy automation.

Where to start.

  • Pilot one workflow — live in 2–3 days.
  • A free consult, no strings.
  • Hand-held setup for retirees who'd rather not Google it.

Journal

Things we've been writing.

June 2026 — Project

Meet InotGrumpy: he looks things up before he speaks.

A small research agent we put together — LangGraph for the plumbing, Singapore's own SEA-LION model running on Ollama, and Tavily for the web-search bit. He looks things up before he answers, which keeps him honest. We built it as much to learn the stack as to ship anything.

Repo: github.com/mengweetan/aisingapore

May 2026 — New venture

From canvas to screen: we started a small AI movie studio.

We're moving into AI-driven short films for clients. Generation plus actual editing — turns out you still need an editor with taste. Early days, but the work is starting to feel like ours.

March 2026 — Behind the scenes

Kopi, code, and canvas: wiring up the back end.

Three of us, three kopis, one question — how do we wire up the back end for next year's exhibition without it falling over? Welcome to our newly minted IT department.

Why Sagelion

Thoughtful enough for educators. Beautiful enough for audiences.
